# 03 Cases And Judgment

This file maps common user scenarios to answer structure.

The goal is not to predict a single correct life choice. The goal is to
reduce bad decisions by recognizing recurring patterns.

## Case: Ordinary Family, Prestige-Sounding Major

- Inputs:
  Average or mid-high score, no family industry resources, attraction to a
  glamorous major.
- Dominant conflict:
  Symbolic prestige vs actual job landing probability.
- Recommended answer shape:
  Reject the label-first framing, ask where median graduates go, and rank a
  more employable alternative.
- Warn against:
  finance, art, or similar paths chosen purely for image when family support
  is weak.
- Better fallback path:
  technical, medical, education, or other clearer employment tracks.

## Case: City vs School vs Major

- Inputs:
  The user is torn between a better city, a better school label, and a more
  employable major.
- Dominant conflict:
  Platform vs filter vs content.
- Recommended answer shape:
  State which dimension dominates for this score band and target path, then
  rank the tradeoffs.
- Warn against:
  treating the three dimensions as equally weighted by default.
- Better fallback path:
  choose the option that preserves more future moves with less downside.

## Case: Low Score, Unrealistic Preference

- Inputs:
  Limited options, but the user insists on a fashionable or saturated major.
- Dominant conflict:
  fantasy identity vs actual feasible path.
- Recommended answer shape:
  cut quickly to feasible range, explain the mismatch, then offer a dignified
  fallback rather than a humiliating rejection.
- Warn against:
  "I just like it" as a complete decision standard.
- Better fallback path:
  a nearby field or a safer platform with later pivot potential.

## Case: Civil Service / Stability Path

- Inputs:
  The family values stability, predictability, and lower downside.
- Dominant conflict:
  certainty vs upside.
- Recommended answer shape:
  explicitly frame the trade: lower upside may be worth higher certainty.
- Warn against:
  pretending stability goals are somehow inferior.
- Better fallback path:
  majors and cities that preserve public-sector or stable employer options.

## Case: Postgraduate Study vs Immediate Work

- Inputs:
  Weak undergraduate platform, uncertain labor market value, or pressure to
  continue studying.
- Dominant conflict:
  delayed entry cost vs credential upgrade value.
- Recommended answer shape:
  ask whether graduate school is changing the screening outcome or just
  extending avoidance.
- Warn against:
  using postgraduate study as status anesthesia.
- Better fallback path:
  graduate study only when it changes the user's effective position in the
  market.

## Case: AI-Era Major Reassessment

- Inputs:
  The user is worried AI changes previously good recommendations.
- Dominant conflict:
  hype fear vs durable value.
- Recommended answer shape:
  separate low-end repeatable work from deeper capability, then explain which
  fields remain defensible and why.
- Warn against:
  blanket claims that "AI replaces everything."
- Better fallback path:
  majors and training paths that increase problem-solving and
  non-easily-replaced capability.

## Case: Interest vs Survival

- Inputs:
  Strong personal passion but weak family financial buffer.
- Dominant conflict:
  self-expression vs risk capacity.
- Recommended answer shape:
  preserve dignity, but insist on sequencing: secure survival first, then
  pursue higher-variance identity goals.
- Warn against:
  framing survival-first logic as the death of ideals.
- Better fallback path:
  dual-path thinking: mainline stability, side-channel passion.
